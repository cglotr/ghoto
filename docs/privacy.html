Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 20, 2026

Overview

Ghoto is a command-line tool that uploads photos and videos from a local directory to Google Photos. This policy explains what data is accessed and how it is used.

Data we access

Ghoto requests access to your Google Photos library solely to perform the following actions:

  • List existing albums in your Google Photos account
  • Create a new album if the specified album does not exist
  • Upload photos and videos from your local directory to the specified album

Data we do not collect

Ghoto does not collect, store, transmit, or share any personal information. All processing happens locally on your machine. No data is sent to any third-party server other than Google Photos via Google's official API.

OAuth credentials

Ghoto uses Google OAuth 2.0 for authentication. Your access token is stored locally in a token file on your machine and is never transmitted anywhere other than to Google's authentication servers. You can revoke access at any time via your Google Account permissions.

Local file deletion

After a successful upload, Ghoto may delete local copies of uploaded files. This operation runs entirely on your local machine and is not related to any data collection.

Contact

This is a school project. For questions, visit the project repository at github.com/cglotr/ghoto.
